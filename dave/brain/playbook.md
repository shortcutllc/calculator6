# Dave's networking playbook — the CEO play menu

How Will networks like a CEO. Every weekly plan picks from THIS menu, working backward from
goals.md (never forward from "who could we email"). Evidence grades: (A) real study ·
(B) practitioner's own data · (C) operator craft judgment. Sourced from the 2026-07-20 research
pass (Ferrazzi, Fralic/First Round, Grant, dormant-ties studies, LinkedIn 20M-user experiment).

## The decision rule (Ferrazzi, goal-backward)

1. Read goals.md: this quarter's 2-3 goals.
2. For each goal, name the SPECIFIC people who unlock it.
3. For each person, pick the play that fits THEM this week (tier, history, live moments).
4. Never suggest "networking" in the abstract. No named person + named trigger = no suggestion.

## Contact tiers (cadence system)

- **Tier 1** (brokers being courted, champions, dream contacts): touch monthly. Mostly no-ask.
- **Tier 2** (warm relationships, past buyers, useful peers): touch quarterly.
- **Tier 3** (everyone else worth keeping): touch ~annually.
- 80% of maintenance is "pinging" — brief no-ask check-ins. (C, consensus practitioner scheme)
- The evidence-based core: there is NO magic touch count. WHO beats how-many-times. (A)

## The plays

1. **Daily 1:1 note** — the drafting lane. One person, one true observation, one bridge to
   Shortcut's story, one question. The baseline, not the ceiling.
2. **Five-minute favor / give-first touch** (A for giver research, C for the 5-min rule) —
   send useful intel, a lead, a genuine congrats, an intro. Ask for NOTHING. Fralic's tracked
   ~80% response rate came mostly from appreciations with "no need to respond." Dave's default
   suggested touch. Cap: bounded giving — never suggest favors that cost Will real hours.
3. **Dormant-tie revival** (A — best-evidenced play on the menu) — people Will knew 3+ years
   ago. Reconnected dormant ties rated as useful as current ties at far less cost. Pick by
   VALUE (status, relevance), not comfort — people instinctively pick who they like, which is
   the wrong list. Quarterly sweep: anyone valuable silent 6+ months. CAVEAT: the weak-tie
   effect is industry-conditional; brokerage is relationship-dense, so for brokers prefer
   strong-tie warmth over weak-tie volume.
4. **Warm-intro ask** (B/C) — ask a connector for a specific introduction with a short
   forwardable blurb. Sustainable: 3-5/week. Track connector fatigue: employees ~weekly,
   investors/advisors ~monthly, client champions 2-3x/year. Double-opt-in always.
5. **Broker hero-making** (C, the channel play) — the default broker touch is an ASSET that
   makes the broker look good to THEIR clients: carrier-fund one-pager, co-branded explainer,
   a "position wellness in your renewal meeting" script, offering a lunch-and-learn under the
   broker's banner. Brokers activate on ready-to-deploy assets, not decks. Asks at most
   quarterly. Their economics: wellness = their differentiation and renewal-retention tool —
   pitch "this wins you the renewal", never "here's our service."
6. **Moment-strike** — a funding round, office lease, new CHRO, RTO announcement, or their
   published content opens a same-week window. Windows from founder_outreach_lane.md: funding
   wk 1-3, new CHRO wait 2-4wk, RTO within days, first People-hire posting 24-48h.
7. **Hosted gathering** (B/C, quarterly bet) — small dinner or 2-hour cocktail format: max 12,
   one conversation, questions in advance, share contacts after. Natural versions: "NYC HR
   leaders" dinner, broker dinner. High effort, hub-positioning payoff.
8. **Conference-as-meeting-engine** (C, proven at Workhuman) — the event is the excuse:
   12+ pre-booked 30-min meetings, 3-4 evening conversations, "attending X in November" as the
   outreach hook, capped side event over a booth.
9. **Founder content** (B) — founder profiles reach ~4-10x company pages. LinkedIn posts on
   RTO/office culture, what 500+ companies' wellness days look like, carrier-fund explainers.
   A multiplier on the 1:1 lane (prospects check the profile), not a standalone channel.
10. **Podcast guesting** (B/C) — buyer proximity beats audience size: 500 ICP listeners beat
    50,000 general. Targets: HR/benefits and broker-audience shows (influence-map.md).
    Expect 3-12 month lag.
11. **Peer community membership** (C) — HR communities, founder groups, EO/Hampton-alikes.
    Outsourced curation. Feed candidates via influence-map.md.
12. **Top-5 dream contacts** (B, Fralic) — a standing list of exactly 5 people who would most
    change Shortcut's trajectory. Alerts on each, mini-dossiers before any meeting, patient
    give-first courtship.

## Banned folklore (never cite these)

- "5-12 touches to close" / "80% of sales on the 5th+ touch" — 1942 survey, n<40. Dead.
- "84% of B2B starts with a referral" — untraceable.
- "Weaker ties are always better" — the causal experiment found an inverted-U; mid-strength
  ties win, near-strangers lose.
- "Givers always win" — unbounded givers finish LAST; the 5-minute cap is the load-bearing part.
- Vendor funnel stats (25-40% intro reply rates etc.) — marketing data, directional at best.
