## IFMA — PLAN OF ATTACK (researched 2026-07-21, all figures verified from IFMA's own pages)

### Why IFMA and not the others
It is the only verified room where a vendor is **expected** rather than tolerated. Lattice bans
us outright; OrgOrg's policy is unpublished; Hacking HR will happily sell us sponsorship but
that is advertising, not standing. IFMA has a membership class built for exactly what Will is,
a sanctioned route onto its stage, and a Philadelphia chapter in Will's own city.

### The membership class Will actually belongs in: ASSOCIATE
IFMA's bylaws define Associate membership as people whose primary responsibilities include
"business development; sales and/or marketing... representation of a manufacturer, vendor,
dealer, distributor; and/or providing facility-related products and services."
(https://www.ifma.org/about/governance/bylaws/article-i/)
**That is Will, precisely.** He does not need to pretend to be a facility manager. Joining in
the honest class is what buys the right to be in the room.
⚠️ Chapter rule: **no more than TWO Associates from any one organisation per chapter.** Not a
barrier to entry, but it caps Shortcut at two people in Greater Philadelphia — so choose them
deliberately.

### Cost (verified at https://we.ifma.org/join/)
- **IFMA + WE together: $337/year.** This is the number that matters for a new joiner.
- WE added to an existing IFMA membership: $99/yr · under-36 $55 · students free · retired $10.
- Prorated from the IFMA join date.
- Chapter dues are SEPARATE and unverified — ifmaphilly.org returned 403 to Dave's fetcher.

### What the money buys
WE newsletter, webinars from global voices, the Workplace Strategy & Leadership Certificate,
local hub activity, conference networking, and a **free Work Design Magazine subscription**.
⚠️ CONVERGENCE: Work Design Magazine is where **Peter Miscovich** publishes — the JLL analyst
who has been on the WORKTECH New York roster three years running. The membership hands us a

## HOW TO ENTER A ROOM — the posture decision (Will's question, 2026-07-21)

### The principle that changes the economics
**Shortcut's product IS an experience, so Will can do the thing in the room instead of talking
about it.** Every other vendor at these events can only describe their offer on a slide. That
single asymmetry should drive every posture decision below, and it is why the default answer is
almost never "buy a booth."

**The receipt: Workhuman.** The 2026 plan forecast 15-25 leads from it. The massage activation
produced **407 signups at ~73% show** — sixteen times the forecast. The activation was not
marketing FOR the product. **The activation WAS the product, demonstrated.**

### The four postures, best first

**1. IN-KIND ACTIVATION — "let us run the wellness lounge." The strongest move.**
Offer the service AS the sponsorship rather than paying cash for a table.
- Solves the organiser's actual problem (attendee experience), which makes it a gift rather
  than a pitch, and gifts are hard to refuse.
- Costs delivery capacity, not marketing budget.
- Puts Will physically in front of the entire attendee flow, not just people who wander past
  a table.
- Frequently negotiates into a discounted or comped sponsorship, and sometimes stage time.
- **The ask, roughly: "Can we bring chair massage for your attendees?"** Nobody has to be sold
  anything for that sentence to work.
- ⚠️ REAL COST: pros, travel, equipment and lost billable capacity. Bootstrapped means this must
  be priced honestly against a cash sponsorship, not treated as free because no invoice appears.
  Atlantic City for three days is not free.

**2. FREE STAGE — do these regardless, they cost only preparation.**
- **Tri-State HRMA**: open call for speaker proposals, no membership required.
- **NYC SHRM**: speak/write form (vendor eligibility unconfirmed — one email settles it).
- **ALA *Legal Management***: contributed articles explicitly open to business partners, unpaid,
  educational only, published 2026 deadline calendar.
- **IFMA chapters**: propose a programme topic to the membership committee.
These are the entry-credential play in its purest form. No money changes hands, so nobody can
dismiss it as bought.

**3. CHAPTER MEMBERSHIP — the compounding buy, for RECURRING rooms only.**
NYC SHRM Gold $3,500 (~35 events/yr) · ALA Philadelphia Copper $1,500 · ALA NYC Liberty $3,000 ·
IFMA+WE $337. You are buying **familiarity over a year**, not a badge scan. The same faces in
June, at the Holiday Party, at the Spring Summit. This is where a founder's presence compounds.

**4. PAID ATTENDEE — scouting, before spending real money.**
Garden State SHRM at $470 early bird. Go once, read the room, meet people, THEN decide whether
to sponsor next year. Never sponsor a room you have not stood in.

### ⛔ THE ONE RULE
**Never buy a sponsorship whose only deliverable is a table.** If the package does not include a
stage, a webinar, an activation, or repeated access to the same room, it is advertising, and
advertising is the posture that gets a vendor tuned out. It is also, per the competitor research,
exactly what our direct competitors have been doing badly.

### Sequencing
Free stages (cost: time) → in-kind activation offers (cost: delivery) → chapter membership
(cost: small, compounds) → paid conference sponsorship (cost: real, and only after attending
once). Most of the value sits in the first two, which is convenient for a bootstrapped operator.
