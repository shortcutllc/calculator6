## âŒ CRITICAL ISSUES FOR RETURNING CLIENTS

### 1. **"First Event" Messaging is Misaligned**
- **Problem**: Returning clients have ALREADY booked events. "First event" messaging feels irrelevant and can even be insulting.
- **Impact**: Creates cognitive dissonance - "Wait, we're not new... why are they treating us like we are?"
- **Psychology**: Returning clients want to feel valued for their loyalty, not treated like first-time buyers.

### 2. **Lacks Differentiation**
- **Problem**: Same messaging for new and returning clients (except hero section)
- **Impact**: Missed opportunity to create urgency and exclusivity for returning clients
- **Psychology**: Returning clients need different triggers - renewal urgency, loyalty rewards, expansion opportunities

### 3. **Weak Value Proposition**
- **Problem**: "Make wellness even easier" is vague and doesn't quantify benefits
- **Impact**: Doesn't create compelling reason to act NOW
- **Psychology**: Returning clients need concrete benefits: savings, priority booking, exclusive services

### 4. **No Time-Sensitive Urgency**
- **Problem**: No deadline, no scarcity, no reason to act immediately
- **Impact**: Low conversion - clients can "think about it" indefinitely
- **Psychology**: Returning clients respond to renewal deadlines, early-bird pricing, limited availability

### 5. **Missing Social Proof for Loyalty**
- **Problem**: Stats section (87% retention, 94% satisfaction) is good but not connected to promo
- **Impact**: Doesn't leverage the "you're part of an elite group" feeling
- **Psychology**: Returning clients want to feel they made the right choice and are getting special treatment
